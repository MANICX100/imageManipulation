If a person is 6ft in height then they are 182.88cm tall, a quantifiable measurement of size that we physically understand.

But digital images are not physical just binary digital data which are represented as pixels traditionally Red Green Blue dots the term resolution refers to the amount of pixels in the image, either represented as:

Horizontal pixels x Vertical pixels

Resolution can also be shown ( as is popular among mobile device manufactures) as PPI/DPI (no that's not payment protection insurance) its pixels per inch the amount of pixels that happen to be in a given space.

This is directly related to the Horizontal pixels x Vertical pixel rating and the screen size, smaller screen sizes the more pixels per inch.

As the dpi rating is related to your own screen size this is not directly related to digital images themselves

The higher resolution ppi count the more detailed or vivid the image appears. If you have 20/20 vision, beyond 300 dpi it is impossible for the human eye to distinguish the individual pixels. Apple used this to market the iPhone 4 as being the first mobile phone that fulfills this criteria.
